What is Two-Factor Authentication?

Two-Factor Authentication (2FA) is a security method that requires two different forms of identification to access an account. This adds an extra layer of protection beyond just a password.

When you enable 2FA on an account, you'll need:

  • Something you know - Your password
  • Something you have - A device that generates or receives authentication codes

Even if someone discovers your password, they cannot access your account without the second factor - the temporary code generated using your secret key.

Why Use 2FA?

  • Significantly increases account security
  • Protects against password breaches and phishing attacks
  • Provides early warning of unauthorized access attempts
  • Required by many services for enhanced security
